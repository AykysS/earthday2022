Why Sustainability?

Fast fashion brands undergo harmful practices that hurt the planet in many ways.

Decrease Pollution

Pollution that comes from production waste (such as dyes, pesticides, and other chemicals) can leak into waterways and harm the surrounding environment and communities.

Reduce Microplastics

The breakdown of microplastics that polute neighboring oceans and other sea life. These microplastics also take hundreds of years to biodegrade.

Preserve Energy

Mass production of clothing is an energy-intensive process that requires petroleum which releases harmful chemicals into the air. Cotton production is a culprit for its heavy usage of pesticides.

H&M

Originally based in Sweden, H&M has become one of the most popular fashion retailors in the world. They operate in over 74 countries and is the second larger retailer following Zara.

versus

Tentree

Tentree is a Canadian lifestyle and apparel brand that focuses on clothing for both outdoor and indoor gear. This brand is widely known for planting ten trees for every purchase.

Environmental Impact

H&M

H&M has tried to improve their company by setting a target to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 2030, pledged to use 100% sustainable material, and created a recycling program. They recently started to use renewable energy and enacted a policy to prevent deforestation.

Despite their improvements, H&M continues to operate under a fast fashion business model and thir practices promote ongoing disposable fashion.

Tentree

Tentree was created for consumers to be aware of the health of the planet. They have already planted 25 million trees and full time employment to underprivildeged communities around the world.

Since 2016, tentree has been a b certified corporation with an impact score of 124.6. The median score for this assessment is 50. 9. Tentree has designed their business model to have a positive impact on the environment.

Price Range

H&M

H&M has a wide variety of prices that comes to their clothing, which can range from $2.99 for a camisole or $347 for a cashemere coat. The average price of clothes comes to $27.61.

Despite H&M’s low prices, they are a fast fashion brand that is not making enough steps to transition into a sustainable business model tat encourages bad enviromental practices, unethical sourcing, and lack of transparency.

Tentree

Tentree’s prices range from $20-$88. They are deemed as one of the most affordable sustainable brands who’s clothing is made from organic, recylced, and circular material that cut down all the waste and water usage.

Shop at Tentree

SHEIN

Shein is one of the largest online fast fashion retailers that goes by the philosophy “everyone can enjoy the beauty of fashion”. Their popularity skyrocketed due to their incredibly low prices and saavy social media branding.

versus

For Days

For Days is a fashion brand that focuses on items that use organic materials, close-loop system, and zero waste. Their mission is to “upgrade your relationship to clothing, creating a life with less stuff and junk, and rather creating a life filled with living.”

Environmental Impact

SHEIN

SHEIN received some of the lowest scores on environmental sustainability. They do not implement meaningful action that reduces their impact on the planet. Their fast fashion business model causes them to mass-produce cheap clothing to keep up with trends and demands.

If SHEIN does implement some sustainable practices, it is hard to assess due to their lack of transparency.

For Days

For Days uses high-quality and sustainable materials to create zero-waste basics. The brand’s mission is to ensure that all of their items are 100% recyclable through upcycling, reusable packaging, and carbon offsets.

They have implemented a SWAP program for customers to swap their clothing in any condition, which then gets recycled and turned into brand new pieces.

Price Range

SHEIN

SHEIN prices range from $5-$150. Their low prices are what made them a popular online fast fashion brand that has taken over social media.

The lower prices may be enticing, but SHEIN has been notorious for their poor quality. The cheap items also cause increased consumption which increases emissions from production.

For Days

For Days price ranges from $6-$89. Their basics can range from $28-$65 with a few higher end times that go up to the $100 range.

With each purchase from For Days, you can feel good about wearing sustainable and recycled clothing that advocates for a circular model that deviates from waste and pollution.

Shop at For Days

Sustainable Fashion Fact

Clothes made of synthetic fibers don’t break down — adding to our landfills.

ZARA

Zara, originally from Spain, is the original fast fashion brand. Zara is owned by Inditex, which operates about 3,000 stores in 96 countries. It is not only one of the largest fast fashion but also works under a highly responsive supply chain.

versus

Known Supply

Known Supply is a fashion brand that focuses on sustainability and ethical production. Each one of their orders is hand signed by the person who made it, in order for the customer to be aware of where their product is coming from.

Environmental Impact

ZARA

ZARA has delved into more sustainable practices with its parent company Inditex, which started a repair and reuse program, and they use recyclable packaging. They have also set a target to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Besides those efforts, there is no evidence that ZARA targets clothing and water waste. They are also not transparent on their track to reach their target goals to reduce emissions.

Known Supply

Known Supply use organic cotton in their material and other eco-friendly materials, which limit the amount of waste and chemicals emitted during production.

Since 2019, Known Supply has been a
b certified corporation with an impact score of 86.9. This means that this brand has designed their business model to have a positive impact on the enviroment, customers, and workers.

Price Range

ZARA

ZARA prices range from $6-$219. The average price of clothing falls in $40.46. Zara’s most optioned price falls in the range of $20-$40.

Zara may have higher prices than other fast fashion brands, but they still follow the same model that does not highlight sustainable practices.

Known Supply

Known Supply falls in the price range of $34-$70, and include clothing for men and women. They push for affordable prices while keeping their sustainable practices and ethical working conditions
